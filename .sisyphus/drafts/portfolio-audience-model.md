# Portfolio Audience Model

This model defines the prioritized visitors, their core needs, and the conversion paths required to turn attention into professional outcomes. It is grounded in the current proof inventory and reference analysis.

## Audience Ranking

1. **Hiring Managers & Recruiters (Primary)**
   - **Goal**: Verify seniority and technical judgment for senior backend or product-engineer roles.
   - **Speed**: High. Needs to validate "Role Fit" and "Proof of Work" in under 30 seconds.
   - **Key Signal**: Experience timeline, resume, and decision-led project narratives.

2. **Peers, Collaborators & Community (Secondary)**
   - **Goal**: Assess technical depth, stack alignment, and ecosystem activity.
   - **Speed**: Medium. Will explore GitHub stats and technical blog posts.
   - **Key Signal**: GitHub activity, tech stack tags, and technical writing.

3. **Selective Client & Consulting Leads (Tertiary)**
   - **Goal**: Determine if the engineer can solve specific business problems with robust technical solutions.
   - **Speed**: Low. Needs deeper evidence of outcomes and trade-offs.
   - **Key Signal**: Case studies (currently "Projects") and contact availability.

## Primary Promise

"A senior backend and product engineer who delivers robust, API-first systems by applying technical judgment and outcome-driven implementation."

## Visitor Questions

- **Hiring Manager**: "Does this engineer have the seniority and architectural judgment to lead our backend efforts?"
- **Peer**: "What is their actual technical breadth, and are they active in modern development practices?"
- **Client**: "Can they translate my business requirements into a reliable, maintainable technical architecture?"

## CTA Hierarchy

1. **Primary (Direct Conversion)**: "Download Resume" and "Get in Touch." These serve the immediate needs of the primary audience (Recruiters/Hiring Managers).
2. **Secondary (Proof Exploration)**: "View Projects" and "Read Blog." These allow the secondary audience (Peers) to validate technical depth.
3. **Tertiary (Network Building)**: "Connect on LinkedIn" and "Follow on GitHub." These maintain long-term visibility with the community.

## Out-of-Scope Audiences

- **Junior Developers & Learners**: While educational content is a secondary signal of authority, the site is not a tutorial platform or course business.
- **General Consumers**: The content is strictly professional and technical; it does not target non-technical users or general-interest readers.
- **High-Volume Agency Clients**: The current proof (placeholder testimonials, lack of service packages) cannot credibly support a "full-service agency" positioning.

## Conversion Actions

1. **Resume Acquisition**
   - **Action**: Download the latest PDF resume.
   - **Surface**: Hero Section (Desktop/Mobile).
   - **Rationale**: The fastest path for a recruiter to move a candidate into a pipeline.

2. **Direct Inquiry**
   - **Action**: Submit the contact form or click a direct email link.
   - **Surface**: Contact Section / Footer.
   - **Rationale**: The primary bridge for interview requests or consulting discovery.

3. **Technical Validation (Future-Proofed)**
   - **Action**: Click through to a "Case Study" or "Implementation Note" from a project card.
   - **Surface**: Projects Section.
   - **Rationale**: Moves the visitor from "scanning code" to "verifying judgment," which is essential for senior-level roles.
