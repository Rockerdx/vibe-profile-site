# Portfolio Reference Longlist

Selection lens: keep only references that teach something usable for this repo’s current state—clearer positioning, stronger proof packaging, more credible contact paths, and more disciplined motion. Current constraints from Tasks 1-3 matter here: mobile/desktop IA diverges, projects are repo-card proof rather than case studies, blog is fragile on mobile, and testimonials remain unusable.

## Clarity-First

### 1. Jack Morgan
- **URL:** https://jackmorgan.com/
- **Audience:** Recruiters, hiring managers, conference organizers, high-trust collaborators
- **Proof Style:** Credibility-forward highlight reel with marquee brands, flagship outcomes, and external recognition
- **Motion Style:** Minimal from the fetched view; the site relies more on strong imagery and hierarchy than animation-led persuasion
- **CTA Style:** Simple top-level paths (`Work`, `About`, `Speaking`, `Contact`) with low-friction navigation instead of a hard sell
- **Strongest Pattern:** Immediate role clarity plus high-status proof above the fold; a reviewer can understand the operator’s level in seconds
- **Weakest Pattern:** Heavy reliance on brand halo and reputation density can feel less portable if the visitor lacks equivalent famous-client logos
- **Transferability:** Transfers cleanly if this repo borrows the fast-scan structure—clear role headline, selected proof, and obvious contact path—without imitating the brand-name flex it cannot currently support.

### 2. Scrimba — How to Build a Web Developer Portfolio That Gets You Hired
- **URL:** https://scrimba.com/articles/how-to-build-a-web-developer-portfolio-that-gets-you-hired/
- **Audience:** Job-seeking developers and hiring-oriented portfolio builders
- **Proof Style:** Hiring-manager guidance anchored in concrete portfolio mechanics: 3-5 projects, live demos, READMEs, case studies, mobile performance
- **Motion Style:** Reading-first article; no motion-dependent persuasion
- **CTA Style:** Educational first, product CTA second
- **Strongest Pattern:** Re-centers the portfolio around proof of work rather than visual packaging, which matches this repo’s current strengths in projects, GitHub activity, and experience
- **Weakest Pattern:** Skews junior/entry-level in tone, so some recommendations need upgrading for a senior engineer narrative
- **Transferability:** Transfers very cleanly as a content rubric: sharpen project narratives, keep project count curated, and treat the site as packaging for proof rather than the proof itself.

### 3. curious.page — How to Build a Developer Portfolio That Gets You Hired in 2026
- **URL:** https://curious.page/blog/how-to-build-developer-portfolio-gets-hired
- **Audience:** Developers trying to turn projects into interviews quickly
- **Proof Style:** Structure-first checklist focused on above-the-fold clarity, curated projects, technical explanation, and contact visibility
- **Motion Style:** Minimal article presentation
- **CTA Style:** Product-led CTA after the educational content
- **Strongest Pattern:** Strong emphasis on first-impression clarity and on presenting each project as problem → solution → technical approach → challenge
- **Weakest Pattern:** The article ultimately funnels into a portfolio product, so some advice is more conversion-copy-driven than evidence-driven
- **Transferability:** Transfers well if used as an IA checklist for the hero and projects sections, but not as a reason to over-template or oversimplify senior-level proof.

### 4. Lee Robinson
- **URL:** https://leerob.com/
- **Audience:** Developers, technical hiring managers, AI/product peers, and readers evaluating technical credibility quickly
- **Proof Style:** Ultra-clear personal-brand homepage with role summary, career lineage, selected writing, code links, and direct contact
- **Motion Style:** Minimal and unobtrusive; the page reads like a polished profile rather than an effects-driven portfolio
- **CTA Style:** Soft but explicit exploration CTA (`read my writing`, `code`, `follow me online`, `Reach out`) with low friction
- **Strongest Pattern:** Compresses identity, credibility, and next actions into a very small amount of screen real estate without feeling sparse or vague
- **Weakest Pattern:** Lighter on project/outcome storytelling than a full case-study portfolio, so it works better as positioning proof than as deep delivery proof
- **Transferability:** Transfers very cleanly to this repo’s hero and contact problems: clearer role statement, tighter credibility summary, and simpler paths to writing/code/contact would all improve scan speed.

## Evidence-Led

### 5. Eleventy8 case study
- **URL:** https://eleventy8.com/case-studies/eleventy8
- **Audience:** Prospective clients, hiring managers, and technical peers evaluating seniority through shipped work
- **Proof Style:** Long-form case study built around problem, constraints, decisions, outcomes, and ongoing iteration
- **Motion Style:** Restrained; the persuasion comes from structure, candor, and screenshots rather than spectacle
- **CTA Style:** Direct but not aggressive (`Get in Touch`) with the work doing most of the selling
- **Strongest Pattern:** Converts experience into proof by narrating trade-offs, operating context, and outcomes instead of listing tools or titles
- **Weakest Pattern:** Dual-audience positioning (clients plus employers) introduces sprawl that this repo should handle carefully
- **Transferability:** Transfers strongly for project/case-study framing and honest senior-operator voice, but this repo should avoid copying the broad service-platform sprawl before proof depth improves.

### 6. Abul Khoyer — Building a Portfolio That Feels Like Home
- **URL:** https://abulkhoyer.com/case-studies/portfolio
- **Audience:** Design-conscious peers, thoughtful clients, and readers who value craft explained clearly
- **Proof Style:** Self-case-study that explains system decisions, design tokens, content model, form UX, and motion restraint in concrete terms
- **Motion Style:** Deliberate and low-volume; motion exists to set tone, not dominate the reading experience
- **CTA Style:** Soft, publication-like navigation with `Work with Me` available but not interruptive
- **Strongest Pattern:** Shows how to make implementation decisions themselves part of the proof, especially around tokens, content modeling, and careful UX choices
- **Weakest Pattern:** Reading-first calmness can under-signal urgency or role-fit for skim-reading recruiters if copied too literally
- **Transferability:** Transfers cleanly for this repo’s “show judgment, not just screenshots” problem—especially by turning technical choices and maintenance discipline into visible proof.

## Editorial/Personality-Led

### 7. Paul Stamatiou
- **URL:** https://paulstamatiou.com/
- **Audience:** Peers, product/design leaders, readers, and long-horizon reputation builders
- **Proof Style:** Deep archive of essays, product commentary, gear posts, travel photography, and a detailed about page that blends career credibility with maker identity
- **Motion Style:** Subtle polish; the core experience is editorial and fast, not animation-led
- **CTA Style:** Soft CTA model based on exploration, RSS, and reputation rather than overt conversion prompts
- **Strongest Pattern:** Sustained authority through long-form publishing and meticulous self-documentation; personality and expertise reinforce each other instead of competing
- **Weakest Pattern:** Weak direct-conversion posture for a recruiter or client who wants a fast “why hire you?” answer
- **Transferability:** Transfers selectively: borrow the substance-first editorial tone and disciplined self-documentation, but pair it with much clearer job-facing navigation than Paul needs.

### 8. Josh W. Comeau
- **URL:** https://www.joshwcomeau.com/
- **Audience:** Frontend engineers, technical hiring managers, learners, and peers evaluating taste plus teaching depth
- **Proof Style:** Personal-brand site anchored in recognizable authorship, interactive technical writing, courses, and a strong About/Contact layer rather than client-case-study work
- **Motion Style:** Polished but restrained; motion supports delight and comprehension without taking over the reading experience
- **CTA Style:** Clear exploration paths (`Articles and Tutorials`, `About`, courses, newsletter, contact) that convert readers into followers or customers without hard-selling immediately
- **Strongest Pattern:** Demonstrates how deep educational content and a recognizable point of view can become proof of seniority and craft, not just supporting content around the portfolio
- **Weakest Pattern:** It is more authority-and-publishing driven than delivery-proof driven, so it should complement—not replace—stronger work/case-study evidence in this repo
- **Transferability:** Transfers well for editorial authority, motion restraint, and content-led credibility. The useful lesson for this repo is not "be a course business," but "make expertise legible through clear, ownable writing and approachable navigation."
